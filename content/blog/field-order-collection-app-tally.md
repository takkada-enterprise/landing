## Why a Field Order Collection App for Tally Matters

A salesman covers 8–15 retailers a day. At each retailer, the conversation goes the same way: what is in stock, what is the rate, can I get on credit, when will it deliver. The salesman without a field order collection app for Tally either guesses (and gets it wrong), calls the office (and waits), or writes the order on paper (and re-types it back at the office at 7 PM).

A field order collection app for Tally lets the salesman answer those four questions in real time from the retailer's counter:

- Stock: live inventory from Tally, by SKU, by godown
- Rate: current price list, including any party-specific discount
- Credit: the retailer's outstanding, credit limit, and available headroom
- Delivery: order accepted, invoice scheduled, retailer's clock starts now

The order does not need to wait for the salesman to come back to the office. It does not need to be re-typed. It does not need confirmation by phone. It is in Tally the moment the salesman taps "save."

## The Five Capabilities the App Must Have

Real distributor-grade field order collection apps for Tally have five capabilities. Anything less is a notes app pretending to be an order system.

### Capability 1: Live two-way Tally sync

The app reads stock, prices, party masters, and outstanding balances from Tally in real time. When the salesman accepts an order, it writes the order entry back to Tally. No CSV exports. No daily refresh. Two-way live sync.

### Capability 2: SKU-level catalog with live prices

The catalog has product names, codes, MRPs, dealer prices, and GST percentages, all read directly from Tally. The salesman can search by name or SKU. The price the salesman quotes is the price Tally has, not yesterday's price list. No daily refresh, no stale exports.

### Capability 3: Retailer-specific credit awareness

When the salesman selects a retailer, the app shows: current outstanding, credit limit, days since last payment, and aging buckets. If the retailer is over their [credit limit](/blog/credit-limit-for-retailers/), the app warns the salesman before the order is taken.

### Capability 4: On-the-spot invoice and e-way bill

The strongest version of a field order collection app for Tally lets the salesman convert the order to a GST invoice on the spot, including the [e-invoice IRN](/blog/e-invoice-on-phone-tally/) and [e-way bill](/blog/e-way-bill-on-phone/) if applicable. The invoice is in Tally, in the retailer's WhatsApp, and on the dispatch list the moment the salesman taps "create invoice."

### Capability 5: UPI collection at the counter

For cash-on-delivery or counter-collection orders, the salesman generates a UPI payment link or QR for the exact invoice amount. Retailer pays from his UPI app. 0% MDR. Money in the distributor's bank in seconds. Receipt synced to Tally.

## How a Field Order Moves From Phone to Tally

The flow inside a single retailer visit:

1. **Salesman opens the field order collection app for Tally, taps the retailer's name.** Outstanding ₹38,420. Credit limit ₹2,00,000. Headroom ₹1,61,580.
2. **Salesman discusses the order.** Retailer wants 50 cartons of detergent, 30 of sanitizer.
3. **Salesman checks stock in the app.** Detergent: 240 cartons. Sanitizer: 65 cartons. Both available.
4. **Salesman adds to cart.** Total ₹74,200. Within credit headroom. App confirms.
5. **Salesman taps "create invoice."** GST invoice generated. E-invoice IRN fetched. E-way bill prepared. Invoice synced to Tally. PDF sent to the retailer's WhatsApp with a UPI link.
6. **Dispatch team sees the order on their screen.** Goods loaded onto truck same day.

End-to-end time at the retailer's counter: 4 minutes. Time saved vs. the paper-order flow: 2–3 days of cycle.

## The Cycle-Time Math: Orders, Invoicing, Collection

A distributor running 12 salesmen, each generating 8 orders a day, processes ~96 orders daily. On the paper-order flow, each order has:

- Day 0: salesman writes order on paper
- Day 1 morning: salesman returns to office, hands paper to accountant
- Day 1 afternoon: accountant types order into Tally, generates invoice
- Day 2: dispatch loads truck, goods reach retailer with invoice
- Day 32–62: retailer pays (30-day credit, applied to day 2 delivery)

On a field order collection app for Tally flow:

- Day 0 at counter: salesman generates invoice on phone. WhatsApp invoice sent. Truck loaded same evening.
- Day 1: goods reach retailer with invoice already received
- Day 31–61: retailer pays (30-day credit, applied to day 0)

Two days collapsed out of every cycle. On ₹3 crore monthly turnover, that is ₹2 lakh of working capital freed permanently, on top of the [DSO compression](/blog/days-sales-outstanding-distributor-india/) effect of same-day invoicing.

## Mistakes Distributors Make With Field Order Collection Apps

**Mistake 1: Picking a generic field-sales app that does not sync with Tally.** The order ends up in a separate database. Re-typing into Tally negates the time savings.

**Mistake 2: Letting salesmen skip the credit-limit check.** A field order collection app for Tally is only useful if the salesman actually uses the credit headroom warning. Otherwise the office is still cleaning up post-facto.

**Mistake 3: Not enabling on-the-spot invoicing.** Order capture without invoice creation leaves a 1-day gap that defeats half the benefit.

**Mistake 4: Forgetting the UPI link.** For COD or counter-collection orders, a UPI link at 0% MDR means the salesman collects on the spot. Without it, the distributor is back to chasing receivables.

**Mistake 5: Skipping the salesman training.** A field order collection app for Tally is a behaviour change. 2–3 hours of structured onboarding per salesman pays back in the first week.
